VISUAL HEIRARCHY

Code

Follow the link to check it out yourself! >>> Code.org

Code.org easily shows Visual Heirarchy with the choice of varying font sizes they use for their content. This section on the site specifically shows what they deem to be important information for the viewer to see. They want to emphisize why they are a great place for people to learn how to code, and draw the viewers attention to the staggering numbers they present by making that the first thing they see when they get to this part on the site.

REPETITION

343 Industries

Follow the link to check it out yourself! >>> 343industries.com

343 did a great job with showing the example of Repetition. They chose to put up a familiar feel as you go through the whole site to make it easy to navigate and look at. As you can see from the screen shot, the entire home page is basically put in this "square filled with info by square" format as they go through and list information about their company.

WHITE SPACE & CLEAN DESIGN

Nike

Follow the link to check it out yourself! >>> Nike.com

Nike is literally built upon the design principle of White Space and Clean Design. Their whole branding is about making clean and pleasing to the eye content, and as you can see from the screenshot, they use their whitespace to make the webpage feel almost as one flowing image that you are looking at. When I look at this website the first thing that comes to mind is balance. Everything feels really simple, elegent, and makes the viewer feel like they are looking at a polished site.
